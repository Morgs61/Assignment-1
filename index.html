Myers-Briggs

The purpose of the Myers-Briggs Personality Test, is to connect the psychological types theory to people’s lives, making it more understandable. It proposes 16 different personality types, based on four major dimensions of personality type (Myers and Briggs Foundation, 2020). The four major dimensions are, Introversion v Extraversion, Sensing v Intuition, Thinking v Feeling, and Perceiving v Judging.

After completing a Myers-Briggs Test (The TypeFinder® Personality Test, 2020), the personality type that I got matched as was ISTP. This personality type is described as tolerant and flexible, someone who is quiet, and waits for a problem to appear before acting quickly to find a range of solutions. Someone who is interested in using logical principles, value efficiency and cause and effect (Myers and Briggs Foundation, 2020).

The Big Five Personality Test

The Big Five Personality Test scores you on five different measures of personality (Truity, 2020).

  • Openness, a person’s tendency to think in complex ways, people who score high on Openness are usually creative, adventurous and intellectual. Strongly related to a person’s interest in art and culture.
  • Conscientiousness, a person’s ability to practise self-control and discipline, people who score high on conscientiousness are usually organized and determined
  • Extraversion, a person’s inclination to seek stimulation from the outside world, usually through attention from others. Extravert v Introvert.
  • Agreeableness, a person’s tendency to put others’ needs before their own, and to cooperate rather than compete. People who score high experience a lot of empathy and get pleasure out of helping others.
  • Neuroticism, a person’s tendency to experience negative emotions such as, fear, sadness, anxiety, guilt and shame. People who score high, are more likely to react to a situation with fear, anger or sadness.

After completing The Big Five Personality Test, I received percentages out of 100 for each measure. Openness 37.5%; Conscientiousness 58%; Extraversion 33%; Agreeableness 71%; Neuroticism 44%. This shows I scored highest on the Agreeableness and Conscientiousness personality measures (Truity, 2020).

Project

Overview

The project which I am proposing, is a health and fitness application / monitor for dogs. This app will monitor a wide variety of things for you dog, so you can make sure your best friend is living their best life. Using a collar, it can track your dogs, heart rate, sleeping patterns, body temperature. It tracks how far you dog moves in a day, how many kilometres they have walked and has a GPS tracker. You can also set fitness goals and connects online to compare to dogs around the world. All which can be viewed in an easy to use app, on you Android or iOS device.

Motivation

The motivation behind creating this application / device is that I know that a lot of people are constantly worried about their dog and are always wondering if they’re okay or not. I’ve had a few health scares with my dog, due to overexertion and with this application it can be avoidable. In Australia, 40% of households with a pet have a dog, the most of any pet in the country, so there is a massive market for an application like this. Using the MIT App Inventor, it would be a great a cost-effective way to create the application that can connect to a collar which is using Raspberry Pi’s another cost-effective solution for this project.

Description

This product requires two technologies, the first being a dog collar which is put around the dog’s neck, which has the ability to measure your dog’s vitalities and sends you alerts when these get to abnormal levels. The different vitals that the collar measures are

  • Heart rate, tracks the heart rate of your dog through sleep and exercise. If your dogs heart rate drops abnormally low or high, you will receive an alert to your smartphone.
  • Body temperature, measures your dog’s body temperature throughout the day. If your dogs body temperature gets abnormally low or high, a smartphone alert is sent.

  • Body position, tracks how often your dog is standing, sitting or lying, which side they’re lying on etc. and the application uses data to determine if your dog may be experiencing pain based on the way they’re positioning themselves.
  • Sleep quality, like human sleeping applications, this does the same for your dog as it tracks how long your dog sleeps for, the quality of its sleep and long it spent in deep sleep. This can also indicate problems associated with your dog.
  • It also contains a GPS in the collar so you can track your dogs exercise, it will track how far they walk, and how fast they run, and it can be used in the application to compare to other dogs around the world. The GPS also can be used to track your dog if he ever gets lost or finds his way out of the house.

The second technology is the mobile phone application for both Android and iOS. It is a user-friendly mobile application which uses graphs to display your dog’s vitalities across the day/week/month/year, so you can always know when your dog is ever feeling off or not themselves.

As stated previously, it can get sent notifications and alerts when certain measures are out of the normal range for your dog. The way this works is that it uses the data it tracks from your dog to create a normal range, and whenever your dog measures a value that is either below or above this range, you will receive an alert.

For example, you have had the collar for about 2 months, so the application has about 8 weeks of data on your dog, it has started to create averages for each measure of your dog. One day your dog is playing fetch at the park on a very hot day. Your dog starts to slow done a little, but you don’t take much notice, then you receive a notification saying, “Ralph’s body temperature is above normal, get him some water and let him cool down”. This would help prevent and risk of danger to your dog, if you didn’t have the app, you might continue playing with him until he gets so hot, he passes out or collapses.

The app also has an online feature, which allows you to compare the statistics of your dog to all different dogs around the world, allowing you to view by specific breed or by country.

Tools and Technologies

To create the Smart Dog Collar, we would use a Raspberry Pi, with abilities to monitor vitalities, via heart rate monitors and temperatures, as well as an inbuilt GPS. It can connect to mobile devices via Bluetooth, to share data to the application. This would be fitted to a leather collar, that comes in a variety of colours.

Using the MIT App Inventor, we would be able to create our application that works alongside the Smart Dog Collar. Connecting via Bluetooth, the application is also able to connect to the internet to allow for comparison to other dogs.

Skills Required

The skills required for this project, is the ability to write a mobile phone application using MIT App Inventor, which has the ability to connect via Bluetooth to another device, as well as the ability to connect to the internet.

Also, someone who can build the special hardware device that can track our dog’s vitalities, using a Raspberry Pi. I think that it would be feasible to find the hardware and software to be able to complete this project, as it is like a lot of health applications for humans. So, finding someone with the skill to be able to create this project would be feasible as there is similar applications and devices out there, that does the same tests for humans.

Outcome

The outcome of this project, if it is successful, would be that we would finally be able to track our dog’s health and activities, to make sure our best friend is in the best of health and happiness. The original problem, of needing a way to measure and make sure our dogs are happy and healthy would be solved, as this project is designed to ensure your pet is always healthy and never feeling uncomfortable.
